Shopify Launch Checklist

A basic checklist to review while setting up your ecommerce project.

Find a product you want to sell.

Before you start selling stuff online you need something to sell. Try to find something where there is an easily identifiable niche. It's always easier to sell a product you're actually passionate about.

You'll also want to think about how the products will be shipped and packaged. How and when your products arrive to your customers is part of the purchasing experience.

Read more: https://www.shopify.com/guides/what-to-sell

Decide on a name for your brand.

Once you have a product you need a name to represent your brand. While you're thinking of names you also want to see what relevant domains are available. After you've decided on a name make sure you register a domain and secure all your social usernames.

  • Pick a name
  • Register domain
  • Secure social usernames

Check usernames: https://namechk.com/

Sign up for Shopify.

Once you've decided on a product and name it's time to sign up for a Shopify account. After you've signed up you can install a free or paid theme for your storefront.

Sign up: https://www.shopify.com

* We're paid an affiliate fee for people who sign up using the above link

Import products and collections.

Once your setup with a Shopify account you'll want to import all your products. Make sure your store includes the following:

  • High quality product imagery
  • Great product descriptions
  • Shipping times and costs
  • Return policy
  • Product collections that are easy to navigate
  • (Optional) Sizing, ingredients, other product features, customer reviews, accepted payment types

You'll also want to make sure you've set shipping rates if you plan to sell physical products

Read more: https://www.shopify.ca/blog/...

Watch more: https://www.youtube.com/watch?...

Tell your brand story.

Your customers want to buy from someone they trust. Tell them about your company and your team using an about page.

You'll also want to create a cohesive visual identity for your brand. Your tyopgrophy, logo, and colour choices should all work together to renforce your brand story.

Read more: https://www.shopify.ca/blog/...

Setup customer support.

Chances are your customers have questions. It's important you are there for them as soon as they need something. Be as available as possible on as many channels as possible.

  • Create a support email account (ex. "support@myproduct.com")
  • Creata a contact form for customers
  • Publish a FAQ page to answer questions
  • (Optional) Link Facebook page and enable the Messenger channel
  • (Optional) Install a live chat application
  • (Optional) Give customers a toll-free phone number for support
Customize customer notifications.

Many of the built in Shopify customer emails are a great touch point to communicate with customers. Make sure you customize these emails and messages to fit your brand.

These emails are a great opportunity to upsell, generate feedback, collect reviews, and grow social followers.

Use a custom domain.

Custom domains help improve customer trust. Make sure you connect a custom domain before launching.

Install Facebook pixel.

Using the Facebook pixel you can track visitors and send targeted ads directed at customers who haven't completed their purchase. Once your pixel is installed you can track events on your store such as 'Add to card' and 'Purchase'.

Read more: https://help.shopify.com/manual/...

Install Google Analytics.

Google analytics is a standard analytics tool to measure your stores traffic. Make sure you enable enhanced ecommerce to get a deeper view of your store.

Read more: https://help.shopify.com/manual/...

Submit sitemap to Google.

Before launching make sure you remove the password from your store. You'll also want to submit a sitemap to Google.

Read more: https://help.shopify.com/manual/...

Double check everything.

Make sure you go over your site one last time and double check everything

  • Spelling and grammar
  • Speed and performance
  • Optimized images
  • Clear 'Call to Action' buttons
  • Shipping
  • Taxes
  • Payment gateway
Time for your first sale.

There's going to be other things on your to-do list that aren't mentioned here. Make sure you've thought about your accounting, finance, operations and most importantly how you plan to get your first sale.
