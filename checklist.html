Shopify Checklist

A basic checklist to review while launching your ecommerce project.

SETUP

Find a product you want to sell.

Before you start selling stuff online you need something to sell. Try to find something where there is a easily identifiable niche. It's always easier to sell a product you're actually passionate about.

You'll also want to think about how you will ship and package your products. How and when your products arrive to your customers is part of the purchasing experience.

Read more: https://www.shopify.com/guides/what-to-sell

Decide on a name for your brand.

Once you have a product you need a name to represent your brand. While you're thinking of names you also want to see what relevant domains are available. After you've decided on a name make sure you register a domain and secure all your social usernames.

  • Pick a name
  • Register domain
  • Secure social usernames

Check usernames: https://namechk.com/

Register domain: https://www.hover.com/

Sign up for Shopify.

Sign up: https://www.shopify.com/

Import products.

Once your setup with a Shopify account you'll want to import all your products. Make sure your product pages include the following:

  • High quality product imagery
  • Great product descriptions
  • Shipping times and costs
  • Return policy
  • (Optional) Sizing, ingredients, other product features, customer reviews, accepted payment types

You'll also want to make sure you have set shipping rates if you plan to sell physical products

Read more: https://www.shopify.ca/blog/8211159-9-simple-ways-to-write-product-descriptions-that-sell

Read more: ttps://www.shopify.ca/blog/8211159-9-simple-ways-to-write-product-descriptions-that-sell

Tell your brand story.

Your customers want to buy from someone they trust. Tell them about your company and your team using an about page.

You'll also want to create a cohesive visual identity for your brand. Your tyopgrophy, logo, and colour choices should all work together to renforce your brand story.

Read more: https://www.shopify.ca/blog/6109584-how-to-use-your-about-page-to-increase-ecommerce-sales

Setup customer support.

Chances are your customers have questions. It's important you are there for them as soon as they need something. Be as available as possible on as many channels as possible.

  • Create a support email account (ex. "support@myproduct.com")
  • Creata a contact form for customers
  • Publish a FAQ page to answer questions
  • (Optional) Link Facebook page and enable the Messenger channel
  • (Optional) Install a live chat application
  • (Optional) Give customers a toll-free phone number for support
